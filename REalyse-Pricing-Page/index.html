REalyse Pricing Page

Redesign of the marketing site’s pricing page to update the pricing structure and also to modernise its look and feel.

Overview

I was tasked with redesigning REalyse’s pricing page when it was decided that changes were going to be made to the company’s pricing packages. We were going to be highlighting more features, redefining each package and changing what was included. Aside from updating the content, it was a great opportunity to give the page a refresh.

The Problem

A few key problems were clear from the beginning, starting with content. Using terms and names that may be familiar to us within the company but not to visitors to the site who’ve never used the platform was one issue. This could seriously affect a visitor’s decision on whether they wanted to contact us about the product. There was also not enough information for users to make an informed decision as not all of our features and services were mentioned in the pricing list.

I liked how the table was organised so people could compare points, but at the same time, it forced them to follow the line of each checkmark to see what feature it was highlighting. It wasn’t as quick and easy as it should have been.

Visually the page looked tired and lacked personality. The top section included an image that seemed out of place and unrelated to the pricing page, and instead of adding character or interest it was taking up space. This project wasn’t part of a larger redesign of the marketing site, so I had to be mindful of the changes I made so it wouldn’t look like a page out of an entirely different website. But going in, I knew I wanted to make it more informative, user-friendly, modern and interesting.

Process

My process started with competitor and general pricing page research. It didn’t need to directly relate to property or data and I found several great pricing pages for a variety of products/services. I was also drawing up a detailed list of our services as an idea of what we could possibly include in our breakdown. With all this information I had a meeting with the Marketing and Sales Team to present some of my research and my ideas for content. From here we outlined what services and features we wanted to highlight on the new Pricing page.

Next, I did some quick pencil and paper sketches of ideas which were quickly brought onto Sketch. I started out with a table style pricing section. After thinking about mobile I thought about keeping the sections as they were in the original design. During my research the separated sections stood out to me, especially when I viewed them on mobile. With two good options, I showed both to several people in the room who all preferred the original.

I continued developing that design on Sketch and created some more low fidelity wireframes. I showed these to both the product lead and the developer I would be working with on this project. This was useful as I received some feedback from the lead and got the thumbs up from the dev.

From here I continued iterating on my designs, adding colour and illustrations to add character, and organising the growing features list to create hierarchy and make things easier to take in.

During the design process, there were a few updates made to the content which I incorporated into the wireframes. I learned that we would be including a section for the API so I had a quick chat about how this would work as part of our pricing structure. I included it in a separately below the pricing table area.

Handoff and Final Product

Throughout my process, I showed my work to different people in the room and always ran changes and ideas by the developer. During handoff, I spent time going through the interactions with the dev (they were clear in the design files but I always like to chat through anything before production starts). There was more back and forth discussions during production which really helped to make sure everything was clear. After the page went through development we ran a round of QA where I submitted a bit of feedback.

Learnings

Content

As a designer in a small start-up I played a large role in defining and creating content for the pricing page. I outlined what I thought should be included in the breakdown of the services and wrote copy. Taking ownership over the project and knowing how to communicate what content I thought was important for our users is an important lesson that I continue to build upon.
